Stable isotope insights into microplastic contamination within freshwater food webs

We quantified the trophic niche of individuals (macroinvertebrates and fishes) to investigate the correlation with the microplastic concentration and features presented in their body - intestinal tract (for fish). We also compared the microplastic pollution (abiotic compartment) and the microplastic contamination (biotic contamination).

Abstract :

Microplastic pollution and ingestion are ubiquitous phenomena in freshwater ecosystems. Here, we quantified the level of microplastic (0.7 mm to 5 mm) contamination for macroinvertebrates and fish within the Garonne river. We then used stable isotope analyses (d13C and d15N) to quantify trophic niches. We then found that microplastic characteristics (shape, color, size, and polymer composition) differ between the abiotic (surface waters and sediments) and biotic (ingested by macroinvertebrates and fish) compartments. The abundance of ingested microplastics increased with the size of organisms in both fish andmacroinvertebrates and tended to increase with trophic position in macroinvertebrates only. Finally, the origin of the resources consumed by fish significantly affected the abundance of microplastics ingested. Altogether, these results suggest the absence of microplastic bioaccumulation in freshwater food webs and the dominance of direct consumption, most likely accidentally.
